Edible Seaweed can be found all around Ireland

One of the most important things to check before you go forage for seaweed is if the beach is clean, check if your seaweed is atached to a rock, it means she is alive and fresh

there is seaweeds that are more tasty than others, and easy to add in salads, other varieties are used on beauty therapy

Sustainable foraging of Seaweed

  • Collect seaweed during the growing season only.
  • Take only what you need, it is illegal harvest seaweed for sale without a licence.
  • Cut half or less of the plant, allowing the rest to re-grow. Don’t Remove the holdfast part, it is the plants roots attached to a rock.
  • Use a sharp knife or scissors.
  • Rinse the seaweed in clean seawater to removing any small creatures which may be attached.
  • Collect from multiple areas, rather than clearing all plants from one area.

Sea Lettuce

Ulva lactuca

The sea Lettuce it is one of the most mild seaweeds, easy to eat and full of vitamins

It's a bright green seaweed with wide, wary fronds, very like lettuce,its atached by a small hodfast.

Grows rapidly in summer

Similar in colour to Eteromorpha(gutweed) but sea lettuce fronds are much wider

Reference Book: The Sea Garden: Aguide to Seaweed Cookery & Foraging, Author: Marie Power

How to pick

  • Gather through summer, plants do get tougher when older.
  • Avoid collecting near seawage or run-off from agricultural land as it may be contaminated.
  • Do not gather in late winter/early spring when plant is fertile.
